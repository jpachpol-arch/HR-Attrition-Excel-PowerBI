# HR-Attrition-Excel-PowerBI
In this projects, I built an HR Analytics dashboard in Power BI to study employee attrition. The goal was to help HR understand where attrition was highest and why employees were leaving.

The dashboard showed an overall attrition rate of 16%, which meant nearly one in six employees were exiting. 

Sales and R&D departments had the highest turnover, and employees aged 26–35 were leaving most often. I also found that lower salary hikes % , under 14%, were strongly linked to higher attrition. 

Roles like Sales Representatives and Lab Technicians were particularly vulnerable.

These insights highlighted risks to revenue, innovation, and workforce stability. 

Based on the findings, I recommend ---
Targeted retention programmes or Sales and R&D department
A review of compensation policies, 
Career development initiatives for mid‑career employees.

This project taught me how to connect data with business impact — not just building charts, but helping HR leaders take informed decisions to reduce attrition and strengthen the workforce.
